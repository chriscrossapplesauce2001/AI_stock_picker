The stock has fallen 33.7% from its 52-week high of EUR 102.70, hitting its 52-week low today at EUR 68.08. The RSI of 18.3 is in deeply extreme territory — statistically, readings below 20 occur less than 2% of the time on large-cap industrials.

- Broader European construction sector weakness: elevated eurozone interest rates weigh on housing starts.
- Volume spiked 2-3x during the selloff (3.2M shares today vs ~1M normally), indicating forced/panic selling.

**What the data actually shows:**
- 2025 revenue: EUR 46.5B (stable, +2.1% in local currencies)

- Net income: EUR 2.88B, flat vs 2024 (EUR 2.84B)
